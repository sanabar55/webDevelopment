The boys recounted their favorite parts of the morning, constantly updating their “expedition chart” with stickers.

After lunch, we headed to the Monkey Jungle. Aziz’s eyes widened in delight as he saw real monkeys swinging from branch to branch, mirroring the antics of his plush toy. A particularly cheeky capuchin monkey seemed to take a liking to us, chattering and showing off with acrobatic flips.

Monkeys are always a crowd favorite at the zoo, and the Pied Tamarin is no exception. During our visit to the Philadelphia Zoo, we delighted in exploring the Monkeys & Primates area, where we had the chance to learn about and observe these fascinating creatures. The Pied Tamarin enclosure was one of the highlights in this section. These small, striking primates are known for their distinctive appearance: they have a dark face, which contrasts sharply with their white-tufted cheeks and upper body fur, resembling a tiny, tuxedoed gentleman. They are one of the smallest primate species, weighing only a pound or two and measuring about 8-11 inches in body length, not including their long tails.
